Your home should be where you’re most comfortable. It’s the place you seek refuge after a long day and the place you can always count on for a good night’s sleep, that is until you hear some thumps and bumps in your floors and walls.

Wondering what goes bump during the night? The answer might lie within your outdated energy systems. If the person on the right looks like you and you’re tired of being tired. Take back your quiet rest-filled evenings and read on. Do it for the sleep!

Older furnaces are prone to numerous duct issues, which can cause loud rumbling noises within your home. Some disturbances can range from 60-80 decibels which has the same sound value as anything between a talking conversation between friends and Friday afternoon traffic, which is not what you want to be hearing when you’re trying to catch some shut-eye.

Sediment build up in water heaters can also contribute to disruptive noise in your home. Similar to buildup you would find in your bathtub, sediment can accumulate quickly. Water can get trapped underneath this sediment, causing it to bubble and give off loud bursting noises.
