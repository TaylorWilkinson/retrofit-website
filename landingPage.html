What is a retrofit?

Retrofitting is the process of modifying something after it has been manufactured.

Retrofitting in the context of energy efficiency for buildings, means making changes to the systems inside the building or even the structure itself at some point after its initial construction and occupation. Typically this is done with the expectation of improving improving the performance of the building, such as reducing the amount of energy that is used to perform a task such as heating. The development of new technologies such as energy efficient furnaces and/or water heaters mean that building retrofits can allow for significant reductions in energy usage.
